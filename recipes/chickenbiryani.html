One-Pot Easy Chicken Biryani

Biryani is the paella of Indian food. To make this one pot wonder, you'll cook rice with British chicken, green beans, aromatic curry spices and sultanas for a touch of sweetness. Delicious!

Ingredients

  • 80g natural yoghurt
  • 2 British chicken breast portions
  • 16g tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 160g fine green beans
  • 11g chicken stock mix
  • 130g basmati rice
  • 30g sultanas
  • 1 brown onion
  • Pepper
  • Salt
  • Vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Before you start cooking, take your chicken out of the fridge, open the packet and let it air Peel and dice the brown onion Chop the chicken breast portions into bite-sized pieces Heat a large, wide-based pan (preferably non-stick with a matching lid) with a drizzle of vegetable oil over a high heat.
  2. Once the pan is hot, add the diced onion, curry powder and a pinch of salt and pepper Cook for 2 min or until the onion is starting to soften.
  3. Meanwhile, trim, then cut the green beans in half.
  4. Add the chopped chicken to the pan and cook for 2 min Stir in the chicken stock mix and add the tomato paste Tip: Add a drizzle of oil if the pan looks a little dry.
  5. Cook for 2 min further or until the chicken is starting to brown Once brown, add the basmati rice to the pan and stir to coat the grains in the spice and oil.
  6. Add 300ml cold water to the pan and bring to the boil over a high heat Once boiling, add the halved green beans and sultanas.
  7. Give everything a good mix, then reduce the heat to low and cook, covered, for 10-12 min or until the water has absorbed and the chicken is cooked through (no pink meat!)
